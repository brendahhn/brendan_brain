## Health

### Hydration, done right: "more" is not better (Run 30)
<sub>source: [`from-health-robot.md`](queue/inbox/from-health-robot.md) · knowledge: [`knowledge-20260721-hydration-more-not-better`](domains/health/knowledge/knowledge-20260721-hydration-more-not-better.md)</sub>

The health robot finished a chapter on hydration and electrolytes. The through-line:

- **[CONC-A] The popular hydration fears are mostly backwards for a healthy, active young person.** The genuinely dangerous mistake is the *opposite* of dehydration — drinking large volumes of plain water during long sweaty efforts can dilute blood sodium (exercise-associated hyponatremia). That's why the modern sports-medicine consensus is "drink to thirst," not "stay ahead of thirst," and why sodium (not just water) belongs in a long-session drink. Aim for **pale-straw** urine, not totally clear — clear can signal mild over-drinking.
- **[CONC-A] "Cut all salt" is calibrated to older, hypertensive, sedentary people** — not a normotensive young person who sweats daily. In the largest outcomes study, the *lowest* cardiovascular risk sat at a **moderate** sodium intake (~3–6 g/day), rising at both extremes. The bigger real gap for most is too little **potassium** (greens, potatoes, beans, coconut water), not too much sodium.
- **[CONC-B] Two myths cleared:** "plain water raises prolactin" is backwards (dehydration raises it; water lowers stress-linked pituitary hormones); and cold-vs-hot water is a comfort preference, not therapy — water re-warms to body temperature in the stomach within ~5 minutes.
- **[CONC-B] Electrolyte-product literacy:** sweat-sodium loss varies ~10× between people and is largely genetic, so there's no universal "right" dose — the personalizing tell is how salty your sweat is (free proxy: white salt crust on a dark shirt, stinging sweat). A clean mix is just sodium + potassium + magnesium in a good form (magnesium **glycinate** is gentle; organic forms beat oxide). Coconut water is good potassium but too low in sodium to stand alone as a sweat-replacement. Formulation note: keep fat-soluble vitamins (D3) *out* of a water mix — it won't dissolve without an emulsifier.

Actionable items are tracked n=1 in the health notebook (session-dose electrolytes over a fixed daily habit; the salty-sweater self-check; provider check on any custom blend's potassium).
