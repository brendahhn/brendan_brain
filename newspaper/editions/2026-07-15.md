## Health  (~290w)

[OBS] Generic research conclusions from the health robot's first two live chapters (Runs 18-19,
brain & cognition). No Brendan-specific health data here. Evidence tiers [A]/[B] ride along.

**Run 18 — focus & the digital environment:**
- [A] **Environment design beats willpower** for a scroll habit: hard access-blocking
  (source-level blockers) + permanent grayscale beat time-limits, tracking, and
  notification-toggling (which alone can backfire). Feeds are variable-ratio reward schedules.
- [A] "Dopamine detox" is a mechanistic myth (abstinence doesn't reset receptors), but the
  underlying behavior change is genuinely effective — right action, wrong story.
- [A] Morning outdoor light + a consistent wake time is the best-supported lever for an early
  cognitive peak; the popular "delay caffeine 90 min" rule has no trial support.
- [A] Raise GABA via movement/breathwork (yoga/aerobic exercise), not oral GABA pills (poor
  blood-brain-barrier penetration); dietary-MSG "excitotoxicity" fear is a myth for the general
  population.
- [A] ACT / psychological flexibility is trainable and ≈ as effective as CBT; values-
  clarification measurably lowers the cortisol stress response.

**Run 19 — long-term brain health:**
- [A] Most brain-health wins are the ordinary base, not supplements: lifelong fitness,
  protected sleep (deep sleep clears amyloid), midlife BP + LDL/ApoB control, not smoking,
  cognitive reserve via novel learning. Lancet Commission: ~45% of dementia is potentially
  modifiable.
- [A] Hearing loss is the single largest modifiable midlife dementia risk factor; treating it
  slowed decline in an RCT — earplugs for loud or cold-water exposure are a real neuroprotective
  move worth starting early.
- [A] Homocysteine gates whether B-vitamins help the brain: high-dose B's slow atrophy only when
  it's elevated. Measure before supplementing.
- [A] Hype that failed the test: the gum-bacteria (P. gingivalis) Alzheimer's drug trial, the
  MIND diet (null RCT), cocoa flavanols (null in well-nourished), and urolithin-A / NMN /
  lion's mane (unproven for a healthy young brain).
- [B] Chronic gut inflammation (IBD) modestly raises later dementia/Parkinson's risk (~20-35%)
  — a modest effect, not the scary single-cohort headline.
- [A] Do BOTH cardio and resistance training; "brain-training" apps transfer only narrowly.
