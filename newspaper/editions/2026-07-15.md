### [CONC] The base beats specialty brain supplements
Most durable brain-health wins are the ordinary base: lifelong cardiorespiratory fitness,
protected deep sleep (clears amyloid; chronic midlife short sleep raises dementia risk),
midlife blood-pressure and LDL/ApoB control, not smoking, and building cognitive reserve via
sustained novel learning. The Lancet Commission puts ~45% of dementia in the
potentially-modifiable column. Do BOTH cardio and resistance training; "brain-training" apps
transfer only to the trained task. (evidence tier A)
<sub>source: [`knowledge-20260713-base-beats-specialty-brain-supplements`](domains/health/knowledge/knowledge-20260713-base-beats-specialty-brain-supplements.md)</sub>

### [CONC] Measure the gating biomarker before you supplement
High-dose B-vitamins slow brain atrophy only when homocysteine is elevated — measure first,
supplement second. General principle across chapters. (evidence tier A)
<sub>source: [`knowledge-20260713-measure-gating-biomarker-before-supplementing`](domains/health/knowledge/knowledge-20260713-measure-gating-biomarker-before-supplementing.md)</sub>

### [CONC] Several hyped interventions didn't hold up
The "gum bacteria cause Alzheimer's" (P. gingivalis) drug trial failed; the MIND diet was
null in a randomized trial; cocoa flavanols were null for the well-nourished; urolithin-A /
NMN / lion's mane are unproven for a healthy young brain. (evidence tier A)

### [CONC] From the 2026-07-12 chapter (focus & the digital environment)
Environment design beats willpower for breaking a scroll habit: hard access-blocking +
permanent grayscale beat time-limits and notification-toggling (which alone can backfire).
"Dopamine detox" is a mechanistic myth but the behavior change works. Morning outdoor light +
a consistent wake time is the best-supported lever for an early cognitive peak. (evidence
tier A)
<sub>source: [`knowledge-20260712-environment-design-beats-willpower`](domains/health/knowledge/knowledge-20260712-environment-design-beats-willpower.md)</sub>

### Martial arts: a no-sparring Muay Thai path fits
[CONC-inferred] Pad work, heavy-bag drilling, shadowboxing, and clinch drilling build cardio,
coordination, and technique with sparring's injury risk removed — a real way to train Muay
Thai, not a watered-down one. Its hip-driven power and footwork overlap with what surfing
rewards; one session doubles as conditioning (~600-1,000 cal). Alternatives cut: boxing
(repetitive overhead load worsens the shoulder surfing already taxes), BJJ (best injury
numbers, but live rolling *is* the training — no-contact isn't really BJJ). Full reasoning
and sources in the task.
<sub>source: [`task-20260711-martial-arts-selection-muay-thai-pads`](queue/active/task-20260711-martial-arts-selection-muay-thai-pads.md)</sub>

### Menu: pot pie, Brazilian lemonade, cream puffs — the night before is the whole game
[CONC] Everything hard happens the day before, leaving cook-day light. **Night before
(~75 min):** braise the pot-pie filling and chill it (cold filling is load-bearing — warm
filling kills puff); bake choux shells (keep 2 days airtight); make pastry cream, chill with
wrap on the surface. **Cook day:** top cold filling with cold puff pastry, egg-wash, bake
~400°F to deep golden; re-crisp shells 300°F; for lemonade, pulse HALF the limes with
sugar-water ≤10-12 sec and strain hard (over-blending drags bitterness from the pith — the
one way it fails), then blend strawberries, stir in condensed milk, serve over ice; fill
puffs within a few hours. [OBS] Sources split on crust (puff vs pie dough) — puff chosen for
make-ahead tolerance. Shopping gaps (pantry empty): chuck ~2 lb, puff pastry, stock,
mirepoix, flour, butter, eggs, milk, heavy cream, sugar, vanilla, strawberries (2 lb), limes
(6-8), condensed milk.
