## 🩺 Health — in plain English
*This week's chapter was microplastics, PFAS & "detox." The bar-conversation version:*

- **"Microplastics are the new lead."** — *Overblown.* Lead has proven, dose-dependent brain damage with no safe level. Microplastics have a scary mechanism and alarming *associations* (found in plaque, in brain tissue) but no proven human cause-and-effect yet — and one 2026 study found lab gloves shedding particles that got miscounted as microplastics, so some "billions of particles!" headlines are partly measurement error. **Do the cheap swaps, skip the panic.**
- **The one free win:** stop heating or hot-filling food in plastic — move it to glass or ceramic first. Costs nothing, biggest bang.
- **"Forever chemicals" (PFAS) are the more legit worry** — decades of human data. Cheapest defenses: filtered tap over bottled water, unwrap fast food off the greasy paper, PFAS-free floss. A real blood panel (not a wellness "toxin screen") is the only way to actually know your level.
- **"Detox cleanses"?** — *Mostly theater.* Your liver and kidneys already do it. The one real bit: soluble fiber genuinely helps clear PFAS, and you already get that from a normal fiber habit — no $60 kit needed.
