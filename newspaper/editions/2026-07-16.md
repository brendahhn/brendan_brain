### [CONC] "High estrogen is bad in men" is a myth (evidence A)
Estrogen is an essential male hormone — it builds bone, governs healthy body-fat and libido, and
supports the heart. For a natural lifter, estrogen-blockers are self-harm: lost bone density, worse
cholesterol, lower libido, for no body-composition benefit.

### [CONC] Testosterone boosters are mostly broscience (evidence A)
One popular ingredient (D-aspartic acid) actually LOWERED testosterone in a trial. Vitamin D and
zinc only help if you're deficient — repletion, not mega-dosing. The real natural levers are sleep,
not under-fueling hard training, and not binge-drinking. This reinforces a cross-domain principle
now on file: **correct a measured deficiency, don't mega-dose a normal level**.
<sub>source: [`knowledge-20260715-correct-deficiency-not-megadose`](domains/health/knowledge/knowledge-20260715-correct-deficiency-not-megadose.md)</sub>

### [CONC] Skip the trendy cortisol and thyroid tests (evidence A / B)
"Adrenal fatigue" isn't a real diagnosis and the salivary/dried-urine kits sold for it have no
validated use — cortisol is managed behaviorally (sleep, caffeine timing, acceptance practice), not
by a test. For thyroid, reverse-T3 has no clinical utility and selenium/iodine aren't warranted at
normal function; a basic TSH + free T4 + one-time antibody is the sensible baseline. "Optimizing
progesterone" in men is likewise broscience.
