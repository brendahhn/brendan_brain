### [CONC] Muay Thai (pads/bag/clinch, no sparring) — it fits; alternatives don't

The no-sparring path is a real way to train Muay Thai, not a watered-down one: pads, bag,
shadowboxing, and clinch build cardio + technique while dropping the biggest injury vector
[CONC-inferred]. Hip-driven power and footwork plausibly reinforce surf rotation. **Why the
others were cut:** boxing loads the already-surf-loaded shoulder (repetitive overhead
punching); BJJ and wrestling are inseparable from live rolling (and wrestling has the group's
highest injury rate, ~30/1000); MMA needs live striking + grappling to be MMA; kickboxing is
the closest cousin but drops the clinch you want — kept as backup. **Watch-for:** shin
irritation and shin-splints are load-management, not contact, issues — build shins by
repetition, not intensity. **Entry:** 2×/week fundamentals for 3 weeks, then 2–3×/week adding
bag + clinch; a gym that makes sparring compulsory day one is a mismatch. **Gaps:** no San Diego
gym was scouted and no health-history consulted — this is planning, not medical clearance.

### [CONC] Menu plan: pot pie, Brazilian lemonade, cream puffs — the night before is the whole game

[CONC] One insight across ~10 sources: everything hard happens the day before. **Night before
(~75 min):** braise the pot-pie filling and chill it (cold filling is what lets the pastry
puff); bake the choux shells (keep airtight, 2 days); make pastry cream (chill, wrap on the
surface). **Cook day:** top cold filling with cold puff pastry, egg-wash, bake ~400°F to deep
golden; re-crisp shells at 300°F for 5–10 min; for the lemonade, pulse *half* the limes with
sugar-water for ≤10–12 sec and strain hard — over-blending drags bitterness from the pith, the
one way it fails; fill puffs within a few hours of serving. **Shopping (pantry empty):** chuck
~2 lb, puff pastry, stock, mirepoix, flour, butter, eggs, milk, cream, sugar, vanilla,
strawberries 2 lb, limes 6–8, sweetened condensed milk. Name a cook day and the prep-night
steps become scheduled reminders.
