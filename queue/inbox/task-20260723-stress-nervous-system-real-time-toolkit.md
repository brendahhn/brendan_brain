## Request

Stress random tips — are any good? Consolidate and research a real-time stress/nervous-system toolkit. Raw notes to work from: HR going up, sweaty and scary is because I'm excited to share, not nervous (reframe). Rock the body at the desk a bit — adrenaline moves us. Forward motion toward anxiety triggers a dopamine circuit — lean in. Move eyes side to side to suppress the fear center (EMDR) ~30s — good for public speaking, not trauma. Very cold shower 1-3 min; 25-30 deep inhales/big exhales, get stressed, then empty lungs, sit 15-60s lungs-empty and find peace — to handle myself in stressful situations. Stop looking at myself on Zoom/Teams. Gaze work: direct gaze, averting gaze, closing one eye, normal conversation — thinking and re-engaging after thinking. Speak to inform and teach, not to impress. Three ingredients for communicating: structure, passion, clarity. Stress mindset: stress is supposed to help us rise to meet demands; how we think about stress shapes how we feel about it — 'stress is helping me step up.' Epinephrine. Stay moving on phone, leg tap. Lean toward camera not away. Increase threshold via exposure therapy (running big mountains, ice baths) — distinctly different practice from in-the-moment tools. Breathing approach in real time. Recovery levers: glass of wine, massage, sauna dip, big meal. Physiological sighs: double inhale through nose (even a tiny second sip), then long exhale — within 1-3 the nervous system shifts toward calm; do cognitive reframing and EMDR after. Hard to control the mind with the mind — use the body to control the mind. Learn to be calm in other states more often.
