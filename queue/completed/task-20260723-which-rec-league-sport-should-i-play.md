## Request

What rec league sport should I play? Tennis? Men's pickup basketball? BJJ? (Also floated Muay Thai.)

## Constraints

Weigh tennis vs pickup basketball vs BJJ vs Muay Thai on: fit around surfing and lifting, injury risk, stress/social benefit, UC considerations, cost, and time. Give a recommendation, not just a survey.

## Research Log

- 2026-07-24 [sonnet] Web research pass (WebSearch, dated sources) comparing tennis, pickup
  basketball, BJJ, Muay Thai (pads), plus soccer/badminton/pickleball, for a ~30s male surfer
  optimizing longevity + social connection + back health + injury risk. Cross-read Health Robot
  Run 33 (social-sport longevity), Run 32 (immune-therapy skin caution), and the completed
  martial-arts task (task-20260711-martial-arts-selection-muay-thai-pads). Epistemic labels:
  [A] strong/multi-source, [B] moderate/single-source-or-inferred, [C] weak/sentiment.

  ### RANKED RECOMMENDATION

  **#1 — TENNIS (primary pick) [A]**
  Tennis is the single best fit for Brendan's actual optimization target. The 25-year Copenhagen
  City Heart Study (8,577 people, Mayo Clinic Proceedings 2018) put tennis at **+9.7 years of
  life expectancy vs sedentary — the largest gain of any activity measured**, ahead of badminton
  (+6.2), soccer (+4.7), cycling (+3.7), swimming (+3.4) and jogging (+3.2). The authors'
  leading explanation is exactly the lever Run 33 flagged: the top sports all require a
  partner/opponent, and **social interaction appears to be part of the mechanism, not a
  byproduct** [A]. Tennis is the highest-longevity sport that is *also* a true recurring
  social/league format — it hits both of Brendan's stated goals at once.
  - Social/community density [A]: league play (USTA, ladders, doubles) is inherently recurring
    and 1-on-1 or 2-on-2 — dense, repeat social contact, the exact "join ONE recurring in-person
    social sport" move.
  - Injury profile [B]: ~80% of tennis injuries are *overuse*, not acute; recreational injury
    risk ~3.49/1000 playing hours. Trunk/lower-back accounts for ~12-39% of injuries and
    shoulder overuse is common. Caveat for Brendan: **the serve loads lumbar extension/rotation
    and the shoulder — and surfing already loads both.** Manageable (two-handed backhand, cap
    serving volume early, don't stack it on a heavy-paddle surf day), but back/shoulder is the
    thing to watch, same pattern the martial-arts task flagged.
  - Surf fit [B]: rotational, hip-driven, explosive lateral footwork — reinforces surf-relevant
    rotational power rather than competing with it.
  - Accessibility [A]: strong in north county SD — Bobby Riggs Tennis Club (Encinitas), La Costa
    Racquet Club (Carlsbad), San Dieguito Tennis Club (9 courts, "premier NC club"), USTA SoCal
    lists "San Diego North County" as its own league area, plus a "Carlsbad Tennis 20s & 30s"
    social group and PlayYourCourt/Meetup ladders. Easy on-ramp for his age bracket.

  **#2 — REC-LEAGUE SOCCER (runner-up, pure social-longevity play) [B]**
  If he wants a *team* rather than a racquet: soccer is +4.7 yrs and maximally social (a whole
  team, weekly). Best team-density and cheapest. Downside vs tennis: higher acute injury vector
  (ACL/ankle from cutting and contact), so a worse injury trade for someone who needs his body
  healthy for surfing. Good #2 specifically because it doubles down on the social lever.

  **Muay Thai (pads) — KEEP as the conditioning complement, not the social pick [B]**
  Already vetted in task-20260711 as holding up on injury-rate and format-fit (no-sparring pads/
  bag/clinch). It remains the best *cross-training/conditioning* option and cross-loads surf
  rotational power — but it is **not a league/opponent social format**, so it does NOT capture
  the longevity-social lever the way tennis/soccer do, and Muay Thai isn't in the longevity
  dataset. Best role: pair it with tennis (2x/wk each) rather than treat it as the "one social
  sport." No grappling = no skin-infection concern, unlike BJJ.

  **Pickup basketball — MIDDLE; free + social but worst injury fit [B]**
  Very socially dense and the most accessible/free option. But it carries the **highest acute
  injury load** of the candidates: ankle (39.7% of injuries) and knee (14.7%) dominate, ~3.85
  ankle injuries/1000 participations for rec players, ~200k basketball ER visits/yr, landing the
  #1 mechanism. Poor trade for a surfer whose lower-body/ankle health is load-bearing for his
  main sport. Fine occasionally; not the recommended anchor.

  **BJJ — DEPRIORITIZED and PROVIDER-GATED [A on the caution]**
  Two independent strikes: (1) the martial-arts task already showed BJJ is inseparable from live
  rolling and joint-concentrated injury (armbar elbow hyperextension, etc.); (2) **and this is
  the decisive one — grappling is a skin-infection sport, and Brendan is on immune-modulating
  therapy for UC.** Skin-infection prevalence in grapplers runs 20-77% (ringworm/staph via skin-
  to-skin and shared mats), and people with reduced immunity are more vulnerable to bacterial/
  fungal skin infection; targeted immune-modulating drugs specifically raise susceptibility to
  fungal/bacterial infection. This lines up exactly with Health Robot Run 32's skin-hygiene
  caution. **Recommendation: do not pick BJJ without clearing it with his GI/prescriber first.**
  Note: immunosuppression is *not* a blanket contraindication to sport — IBD guidance actively
  encourages exercise — the issue is specifically the grappling skin-contact vector, which
  tennis/soccer/Muay-Thai-pads avoid entirely.

  **Badminton / pickleball — honorable mentions [B]**
  Badminton is #2 in the longevity data (+6.2) and social, but has surprisingly high lower-limb/
  ACL injury rates from smash-landing mechanics, and leagues are thinner in NC San Diego.
  Pickleball is the most socially frictionless on-ramp (strong wellbeing/mood evidence, very easy
  to meet people) with mostly minor/overuse injuries — a good low-stakes *entry* if tennis feels
  like too big a commitment, but it lacks the hard longevity numbers tennis has.

  ### BOTTOM LINE
  Play **tennis** as the one recurring social sport (best longevity + social + NC-SD access),
  optionally **keep Muay Thai pads as the conditioning complement**. Choose **rec soccer** over
  tennis only if he'd rather be on a team. **Hold off on BJJ pending a prescriber conversation**
  about grappling skin-infection risk on immune-modulating therapy. Pickup basketball is fine
  socially but is the worst injury trade for a surfer.
