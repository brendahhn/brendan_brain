## Findings

### The cooking article (Managing Editor: this is the concierge-section draft, ~300w)

**Menu plan: pot pie, Brazilian lemonade, cream puffs — the night-before is the whole game.**

[CONC] Three dishes, one insight from comparing ~10 sources: everything hard happens the
day before, leaving cook-day light.

**Night before (≈75 min active):** (1) Braise the pot-pie filling — brown chuck in batches,
deglaze, braise ~90 min until tender — and refrigerate. Cold filling is load-bearing: warm
filling under pastry kills puff (thecozyapron.com). (2) Bake choux shells; store airtight
at room temp (theflavorbender.com — shells keep 2 days). (3) Make pastry cream; chill with
plastic wrap pressed on the surface (natashaskitchen.com).

**Cook day:** ~T–75m: top cold filling with COLD puff pastry, egg wash, bake ~400°F until
deep golden. ~T–45m: re-crisp shells 300°F ×5-10 min, cool (nigella.com). ~T–30m: lemonade —
wash limes (waxed skins turn bitter), trim ends, wedge; pulse HALF the limes with sugar-water
5 pulses / ≤10-12 sec, strain pressing hard, repeat, then pulse strawberries with the last
of the sugar-water; stir in condensed milk at the end. Over-blending drags bitterness out
of the pith — the one way this drink fails (icecreaminspiration.com, cookingclassy.com).
Serve over ice immediately. ~T–15m: fill puffs (fill ≤ a few hours ahead or they soften),
dust, done.

**Shopping gaps** (pantry empty in the Brain — full list): chuck roast ~2 lb, puff pastry,
stock, onion/carrot/celery, flour, butter, eggs, milk, heavy cream, sugar, vanilla,
strawberries (2 lb: drink + puffs), limes (6-8), sweetened condensed milk.

[OBS] Sources disagree on pot-pie crust: puff pastry (most) vs pie dough (gooddinnermom) —
puff chosen for make-ahead tolerance. Health-guidance fit: no guidance rows exist yet;
