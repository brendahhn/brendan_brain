Hearing loss is the single largest MODIFIABLE risk factor for midlife dementia, and a major
trial showed treating it slowed cognitive decline in higher-risk older adults — so protecting
hearing is protecting the brain, not vanity. Practically: noise-induced loss is permanent, the
WHO rule of thumb is ~80 dB for up to ~40 hrs/week with every +3 dB halving the safe time, and
for a lifelong surfer vented surf earplugs also prevent "surfer's ear" (bony ear-canal growths
that reach ~70-80% lifetime certainty without protection and eventually need surgery). Cheap
gear now vs. an operation and cognitive risk decades out. Flagged by health-robot (Run 26,
eyes/ears/bones chapter). Not yet Brendan-confirmed.
