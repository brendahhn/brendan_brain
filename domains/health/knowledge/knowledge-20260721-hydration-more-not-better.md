For hydration, "more" is not better past adequacy: the body defends water balance tightly, so
the real risks are symmetric — too little AND too much. Over-drinking plain water during long
sweaty efforts can dilute blood sodium (exercise-associated hyponatremia), which is why the
modern sports-medicine consensus is "drink to thirst," not "stay ahead of thirst," and why
sodium belongs in a long-session drink. Practical readout: "drink to thirst + pale-straw urine"
(not totally clear) beats any fixed ounce target. General principle worth carrying cross-domain.
