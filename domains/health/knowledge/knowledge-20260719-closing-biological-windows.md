Some health assets are built during a biological window that then closes, so prevention timing
matters as much as the intervention. Peak bone mass is largely set by ~30 (men build ~90% by
~age 20, peak ~26); after that it is maintenance, not building — and IBD plus steroid exposure
actively lowers bone density while the usual meds do not protect the skeleton, so for a younger
adult with IBD the bone-building inputs (resistance + impact exercise, adequate vitamin
D/calcium/protein, and crucially not under-eating) are a use-it-or-lose-it window. General
principle worth carrying cross-domain: the cheapest interventions are the ones done inside the
open window. Flagged by health-robot (Run 26, eyes/ears/bones chapter). Not yet Brendan-confirmed.
