For breaking a phone/scroll habit (and habit change generally), the evidence favors
environment design over willpower: hard access-blocking (source-level app/internet blockers)
plus permanent grayscale beat time-limits, tracking, and notification-toggling — which alone
can backfire. Social feeds are variable-ratio reward schedules, engineered to defeat
self-control, so structural friction outperforms intention. Flagged by health-robot (Run 18)
as a recurring cross-domain principle. Not yet Brendan-confirmed.
