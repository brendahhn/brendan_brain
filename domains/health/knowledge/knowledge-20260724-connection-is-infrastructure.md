"Connection is infrastructure": treat recurring in-person social contact as a health and
longevity input on par with exercise, not a leisure extra. Across 148 studies (>300k people)
stronger social relationships predicted ~50% higher survival odds — and DEPTH beats headcount
(a couple of genuinely close friends > a big shallow network; "do you live alone" barely
predicts). Highest-leverage single move: join ONE recurring in-person SOCIAL sport — over 25
years, socially-interactive sports beat solo cardio for life expectancy (tennis +9.7 yrs,
badminton +6.2, soccer +4.7 vs jogging +3.2), the socialization itself appearing to be part of
the mechanism. Loneliness has real biology (CTRA: up-regulated inflammatory / down-regulated
antiviral genes) and conflict is physiological (hostile conflict slowed wound-healing ~40%), so
connection is a genuine anti-inflammatory lever. Two more durable principles: build systems
ANTIFRAGILE (a small non-negotiable core + pre-built backups so a life transition shrinks the
system on purpose instead of collapsing it), and the partnered/social version of any habit tends
to out-perform the solo version on adherence. Flagged by health-robot (Run 33). Not yet Brendan-confirmed.
