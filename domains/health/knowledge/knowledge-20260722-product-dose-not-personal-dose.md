A formula sold to strangers should be more conservative than an individually-tuned regimen:
"product dose ≠ personal dose." Corollaries from the sleep-tea/powder formulation chapter:
(1) for a sensitive gut (IBS/IBD), high-intensity plant sweeteners (monk fruit > stevia) beat
sugar-alcohol polyols (erythritol/xylitol/sorbitol), which ferment in the colon — a 2025 mouse
study showed sorbitol worsened colitis; monk fruit is the cleanest and possibly mildly
prebiotic. (2) Magnesium form matters: glycinate is gentlest but dissolves poorly; citrate
dissolves clear but is more laxative. (3) "Clean-label is a business moat, not just a health
choice" — the instinct that avoids questionable additives is also a market differentiator; a
melatonin-free sleep product is an honest selling point given the category's label-accuracy
problems. Research-integrity note: this run again caught a mis-numbered user-supplied citation
