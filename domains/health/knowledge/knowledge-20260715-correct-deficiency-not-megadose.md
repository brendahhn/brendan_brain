A nutrient or hormone lever generally helps only up to repletion, not beyond. Vitamin D and
zinc raise testosterone only in someone who is deficient — repletion works, mega-dosing does
not add benefit and can harm (e.g. D-aspartic acid, a popular "test booster," actually
LOWERED testosterone in a trial). The general principle: correct a measured deficiency toward
normal, rather than pushing a normal level higher. Reinforced by health-robot Run 21
(hormones chapter), rhyming with the "measure the gating biomarker before supplementing"
principle. Cross-domain (applies beyond health to any "more is better" dosing intuition).
