For the plastics / chemicals / "toxins" genre, proportion is everything: the biggest wins are
cheap and boring (don't heat or hot-fill food in plastic, filter tap water over bottled,
choose "fragrance-free / free-of" labels), the scary numbers are often inflated by measurement
artifact (2026 study: lab gloves shed residue miscounted as microplastics) or by
extreme-exposure cohorts, and paid "detox / cleanse" products are the low-value trap — the liver
and kidneys are the detox system. Two operational rules-of-thumb: the LABEL ("fragrance-free /
free-of") outperforms the PRICE ("premium clean brand") — a human study cut urinary phthalate/
paraben levels 27-45% in just 3 days of switching, so body burden is reversible fast; and
microplastics are a real-but-over-hyped concern, NOT "the new lead" (lead has proven no-safe-
level dose-dependent harm; microplastics so far have mechanism + associations but no proven
human causation). Consolidates health-robot Runs 28 (products) and 29 (microplastics/PFAS/detox);
rhymes with "base beats specialty" and "correct a deficiency, don't mega-dose." Not yet
Brendan-confirmed.
