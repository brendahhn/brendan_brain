The highest-value environmental-health levers are one-way (hard-to-reverse) decisions made at
purchase or lease time — which apartment (damp/moldy housing has the clearest respiratory harm
and is unfixable after you sign), which stove, which cookware — so the moment to think hard is
BEFORE committing, not after. The companion principle from the same chapters: the boring, cheap
stuff has the real evidence (clean air, no mold, particulate filtration, filtered tap water),
while the exotic/expensive stuff (structured water, detox gadgets, panic-tossing intact pans)
is mostly evidence-free — spend effort where the data is. The decision-timing half generalizes
well beyond health (vehicles, housing, any irreversible purchase): front-load diligence onto
one-way doors. Flagged by health-robot (Run 27b, environmental chapter). Not yet Brendan-confirmed.
