For an autoimmune condition on immune-modulating therapy, "immunostimulant ≠ good": echinacea
and elderberry are immunostimulants (echinacea is formally EMA-contraindicated in autoimmune
disease) and work against the point of an immunomodulator — avoid them. Mechanism-over-vibes:
the S1P-modulator class doesn't "shut off" immunity — it traps naïve/central-memory lymphocytes
in lymph nodes while leaving effector-memory T cells and the entire innate immune system
circulating, so front-line defense against already-encountered germs stays largely intact
(reassuring on infection while still explaining the real risks: viral reactivation, long-term
skin-cancer surveillance). Best evidence-backed cold tool = zinc ACETATE LOZENGES (~33% shorter
colds, local action, started <24h) — not swallowed pills or fizzy drinks. Vitamin D modestly
prevents respiratory infection with DAILY dosing in the deficient (not big boluses); vitamin C
does not prevent colds. Recurring principle: provider-gate any supplement/med/vaccine change.
